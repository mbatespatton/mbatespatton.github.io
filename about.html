about

Hey, I am Madeleine.

I am a UX researcher passionate about you. Especially those of you who aren’t always at the top of a designer’s mind.

I never knew how much design impacted my life until I was diagnosed with a genetic, connective tissue disorder. The simplest tasks, such as opening a can of marinara sauce, became nearly impossible without the use of accessible products.

As a UX Research Intern, I was tasked to identify opportunities to expand access for disabled students for online learning. My personal experiences coupled with a degree in Cognitive Science, allowed me to provide a unique and missing perspective on the project.

I am committed to making accessibility mainstream; often challenging myself and others to examine how we can design accessible products without sacrificing aesthetics or simplicity.

These days, you will often find me inventing new features for applications or discussing usability improvements with unsuspecting (and unwilling) strangers.

I am always excited to learn new things or collaborate on projects that inspire me. Check out my resume, and LinkedIn to learn more about my background!
